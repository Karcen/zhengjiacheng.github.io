About Me

Thank you for visiting my website. I am Jiacheng Zheng (Karcen Zheng), currently an undergraduate student (Class of 2022) majoring in Bioengineering at the Marine College, Shandong University (Weihai). I previously studied Chemical Engineering at Dalian University of Technology (2019‚Äì2021), and participated in a dual-degree program in International Politics and International Economics at the Northeast Asia Studies, Shandong University (2022‚Äì2023).

I have worked as a research assistant at the Center for Data Analysis on Area and Country Studies, Institute of International Studies, Shandong University, under the supervision of Prof. Yuan Li (2023‚Äì2025), and previously under Dr. Jamal Khan.

From May to August 2024, I joined the Climate Change Economics and Low Carbon Transition team at University College London, Bartlett School of Sustainable Construction, supervised by Prof. Zhifu Mi and Dr. Haoran Zhang, contributing to the development of the IPCC database.

In 2025, I worked as a research Intern at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology for the Hong Kong Generative AI Research & Development Center (HKGAI) project. I was involved in deploying the HKGAI large language model for the Hong Kong government and collaborated on data alignment with Jiaming Ji and Boyuan Chen from Peking University.

I am a recipient of the National Scholarship (2020) and the National Encouragement Scholarship (2023). I am also a member of the International Input-Output Association (IIOA, 2025-2026), the Chinese Society of Optimization, Systems Engineering, and Economic Mathematics(2023-2024), and the Beijing Society for Graphics and Image(2023-2024).

My academic contributions include 6 SCI/SSCI-indexed publications (one first-authored and one submitted to NeurIPS 2025), a book chapter translation (Innovation and Regional Growth in the EU, Chapter 3, Beijing Economic Management Press, 2024), and acknowledged in the English textbook Wind from the East: How does the Belt and Road Initiative connect continents to unleash economic potential? (Peter Lang). I also participated in a National Social Science Fund project on the evaluation of soft connectivity under the Belt and Road Initiative (2021).

If you have any questions or would like to get in touch, please feel free to contact me via email.
